Where it all began...

The Power of a High School CS Class

July 28, 2024 Tanvi Yamarthy

Five year old me would be so surprised seeing where I'm at right now. I was the last person to want to work in "IT". After growing up in an Asian household with both parents working desk jobs as software engineers, I never could see myself wanting the same. They encouraged me to do something different(aka be a doctor), and so going into high school thats what I thought I was going to be. It was only until I took my first biology class that I realized I absolutely hated the subject itself. I was bored out of my mind in classs and for some reason, the endless theoretical memorization could not stick with me. It wasn't until I took AP Computer Science as an 11th grader who knew barely anything about coding did I realize the joy I found in it. Everyday, I would come into class stimulated and ready to learn about object orientated programming and the importance of computer science in the real world. That class constantly challenged me and never bored me, and that was the very first seed that was planted. It was like an inkling ready to spurt and grow. And starting off my fall semester of freshman year taking an introductory Java course only solidified that liking for programming and learning about the tech industry. Two years later, I'm continuing to expose myself to new opportunities, learn new skills at my internship, and take courses that challenge me. Follow me along on my journey!
